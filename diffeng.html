The Difference Engine was created by inventor and polymath, Charles Babbage (born December 1791 died October 1871),
and Ada Lovelace (born December 1815 died November 1852).
It is an 'automatic mechanical calculator designed to tabulate polynomial functions'.

The engine was constructed from the frustration that the human computational mind was far from perfect,
and that the world was in need of something that would calculate anything perfectly.

Babbage's Difference engine was one of the first ever recognised computers however it is not an electronic computer and it does not have a screen. It is a mechanical calculator that can work out more advanced and higher quantity calculations than a normal calculator today.
Babbage approached the project very seriously. Charles Babbage was not an engineer, but a mathematician - so he planned and drew the design and a quality mechanic constructed it.
Back then, people only carried out calculations to 6 decimal places but the Difference Engine carried calculations out to 20 - 30 decimal calculations. The digits and decimals are represented by positions on toothed wheels, rather than the binary digits. When one of the toothed wheels turned from 9 to 0, it caused the next wheel to advance one position, carrying the digit.
